The decisions

In dependency order. Decision 1 unblocks everything else.

D1

Trap-rate: arm now, or tune first?

Fused recall raises trap@5 from ~0.02 to 0.15–0.20 — the semantic lane surfaces engineered lookalikes the lexical lane never matched. The mitigation knobs are config-only: rrf_k (how fast rank fusion decays) and knn_limit (how many vector candidates enter fusion). This gates the re-arm commit (D2).

Arm now

  • 4–11× relevance lift ships immediately instead of sitting on a shelf
  • Trap exposure is overstated by the eval (corpus-wide vs namespace-scoped production)
  • Knobs are config-only — tuning later requires no code change and no new arc
  • Armed tripwire starts protecting the new quality level right away

Arm now

  • Ships a known 7–10× trap-rate regression into the armed baseline
  • If a tune would've recovered traps cheaply, you re-arm twice (two [bench-update] human commits)
  • No data yet on whether rrf_k/knn_limit trade traps against recall favorably

Tune first

  • One sweep (~6–9 fused-report runs) gives the trap-vs-recall curve before committing
  • Re-arm happens once, at the chosen operating point

Tune first

  • Delays everything downstream (D2 → D3 → push)
  • Risk of trading away real recall to suppress synthetic traps that production scoping already mitigates
  • Tuning against the fictional corpus may overfit to its engineered traps
My recommendation — hybrid, weighted "arm" Run a cheap bounded sweep first — 3 values of rrf_k × 2 of knn_limit, fixture provider, ~30 min wall-clock since it's config-only re-runs of --fused-report. If any point cuts trap@5 below ~0.10 while keeping ≥90% of the nDCG lift, arm there; otherwise arm at defaults. This is "tune first" in letter but "arm now" in spirit: it's one bounded measurement, not an open-ended tuning arc, and it means you only sign one re-arm commit. If you'd rather skip even that, arming at defaults is defensible — the production namespace scoping is a real mitigation, not a rationalization.
